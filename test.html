How to use in your site

  1. Copy the <svg> <defs> block into your app shell (e.g., _app.tsx or your root layout). It defines #bk-rosette.
  2. Place the mark anywhere via: <svg width="64" height="64"><use href="#bk-rosette" /></svg>.
  3. Change colors by editing --logo-start and --logo-end CSS variables.
  4. SVG favicon (modern browsers): 
    <link rel="icon" type="image/svg+xml" href="data:image/svg+xml;utf8,<svg xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg' viewBox='-64 -64 128 128'><defs><linearGradient id='g' x1='0' y1='0' x2='1' y2='1'><stop offset='0%' stop-color='%23fecaca'/><stop offset='100%' stop-color='%23c7d2fe'/></linearGradient></defs><g transform='rotate(22.5)'>{P}</g></svg>" />
    Replace {P} with the eight rotated rounded-rect petals, or export the full SVG from above.

Tip: For a Polish nod in the wordmark, consider “Mój zapał” above the nav, or just “zapał”.
